The Arrangement of Particles in Matter

The Arrangement of Particles in a Solid

In a solid, the particles are usually arranged regularly and packed closely together.

The particles are only able to vibrate in fixed positions; they cannot move around.

The particles have strong attractive forces between them, which keep them together.

The Arrangement of Particles in a Liquid

In a liquid, the particles are touching, but some gaps have appeared.

This is why liquids are less dense than solids.

The forces between the particles are less effective, allowing the particles to move around each other.

The particles in a liquid are arranged randomly.

The Arrangement of Particles in a Gas

The particles in a gas are moving randomly at high speeds in all directions.

In a gas, the particles are much further apart, and there are (almost) no forces attracting them.

The kinetic energy of particles in different states of matter:

  • Solid < Liquid < Gas
