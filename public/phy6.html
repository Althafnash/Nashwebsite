Simple Machines

Some of the simple machines we use are:

  • Lever
  • Inclined plane
  • Wheel and axle
  • Pulley

Parts of a simple machine:

  • Load
  • Effort
  • Fulcrum

The force applied downward is known as effort.

The weight lifted is known as load.

The point at which the machine is in contact is known as the fulcrum.

The arm from the load to the fulcrum is known as the load arm.

Calculation on Simple Machines

Calculations on the Lever

The advantage of the machine is calculated by the ratio of load to effort, which is known as mechanical advantage.

Mechanical advantage = load / effort

The velocity ratio is the ratio of the displacement of the load to the displacement of the effort.

Work done on the machine is known as work input.

Work input = effort * displacement of effort

Work done by the machine is known as work output.

Work output = load * displacement of load

Efficiency of a machine:

  • Efficiency = work output / work input
  • Efficiency = load * displacement of load / effort * displacement of effort
  • Efficiency = load / effort * distance traveled by load / distance traveled by effort
  • Efficiency = mechanical advantage * 1 / velocity ratio
  • Efficiency = mechanical advantage / velocity ratio * 100%

We can use the same formulas for the inclined plane.

In the wheel and axle, the calculation for the velocity ratio changes:

Length of the handle equals the radius of the circle = r

If the diameter of the circle is 2r, then the circumference is 2r * π

If the radius of the cylinder stem is r2, then the diameter is 2r2.

The distance that the load is lifted for one turn of the handle is 2πr2.

The velocity ratio of the wheel and axle = circumference of the handle in one turn / circumference of the wheel.

  • = 2πr1 / 2πr2
  • = r1 / r2
  • = radius of the wheel / radius of the axle

In the pulley, the velocity ratio and the efficiency change:

Mechanical advantage:

  • = effort / load
  • = 12N / 12N
  • = 1N

The efficiency = mechanical advantage / velocity ratio.

  • = 1 / 1 * 100%
  • = 100%

In a pulley, the efficiency is always 100%.

The pulley in a well is known as a stationary pulley.

The pulley in a bicycle riding fast along a horizontal road has to climb up the hill.

This is known as the pulley system.
