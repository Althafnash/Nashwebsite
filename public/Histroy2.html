The Social Organization of Ancient Society

Ancient people had a well-organized hierarchy.

There were two types of hierarchy:

  • Vertical hierarchy
  • Horizontal hierarchy

The Ancient Society of Sri Lanka

There was no technology of communication to rule far away,

so to rule remote areas, they used ministers to govern these regions.

In the Vallipuram Golden Plate, there is a mention of a minister named Risigiy who ruled the Jaffna area during the reign of King Vasabha.

  • Dasagam Ethan — Head of ten villages

If there was a problem regarding alms given to the temple, the Dasagam Ethan should gather and solve the issue.

  • Vejasal — Hospital
  • Veja — Doctor

King Buddhadasa built a hospital.

King Mahinda V acted to provide everything required for the hospital in the province.

During the period of King Kashyapa IV, a dangerous fever spread in and around Anuradhapura.

There were three main institutions which governed the country:

  • Legislature
  • Executive
  • Judiciary

The Legislature was the king's court.

The Executive implemented the laws.

The Judiciary provided justice to the countrymen.

  • Eka Asthana — King's court
  • Samaya — Agreement
  • Wath Himiyan Wadala — King's decree

The officers named Udhaya in Menikgamuwa and Sena in Nikawella had come representing the council to offer a plot of land called Kerelegama to the nunnery called Mindarama.

  • Maha Lekam — Great writer / Great secretary

They were responsible for recording all government affairs and taking necessary steps to preserve them.

King Kashyapa IV built a hospital for patients who were infected by the dangerous fever that spread during his time.

  • Thimbirige — Maternity hospital

King Upatissa I built a maternity hospital for pregnant women.

Our kings also mentioned maternal and trade affairs with other countries.

  • King Gajabahu I arrived in Chera State in South India to participate in a ceremony where the Pathini Devalaya was declared open.
  • King Kanatissa sent ambassadors to Romekuladeshaya (Rome) to fetch glass pebbles for a pooja performed in the Ruwanwelisaya Maha Seya.
  • During King Agbo's period, to strengthen trade affairs with China, 24 missions were sent.
  • Sri Lanka had marital relations with neighboring countries such as Kalinga and Pandya to strengthen political power.
  • King Vijayabahu I appointed the Velakkara army for the protection of the Polonnaruwa Tooth Temple.
  • After the 10th century, our kings had mutual relations with Arabian countries to strengthen trade affairs.

Economy

There were three main sectors in the economy:

  • Agriculture
  • Industries
  • Trade

Some of the main sectors of agriculture:

  • Chena cultivation
  • Paddy cultivation
  • Animal husbandry

The ways of preparing a chena cultivation:

  • A suitable land for chena is prepared for growing grains and vegetables after choosing a plot of land from a jungle.
  • The owner shouts to chase away the animals.
  • Nawa Deli Hena — A new chena that has been cleared by setting fire to it.
  • Danu Weta — The fence that was made around the chena to protect it against wild animals.
  • Pela — The watch hut which was built on top of a tall tree.

The farmer stayed up sleepless at night to protect the chena from wild animals.

The Nawa Deli Hena was so called because it produced nine plants, namely:

  • Finger millet (Kurakkan)
  • Kalu
  • Undu
  • Green gram
  • Corn (Iringu)
  • Millet (Thanahal)
  • Asamodagam
  • Amu

The normal method practiced in chena cultivation was abandoning a chena after cultivating in it twice. Then it grows wild again, and therefore, it does not harm the environment.

  • Kanaththa — The deserted chena which was reused after being abandoned.
  • Ketha, Kubura — The places where paddy was cultivated.

Most paddy cultivation was done using irrigation. Rainwater was reserved during the rainy season and carried to paddy fields through canals.

Paddy cultivation was done twice a year.

There are 03 seasons:

  • Yala season
  • Maha season
  • Keda season

Some of the strategies used to gather water:

  • Gathering rainwater
  • Taking water to paddy fields through canals by putting a barrier across a natural river

"Not a single drop of water that falls off the sky should be sent to the sea without properly using it." — King Parakramabahu I

Rearing cattle was a major activity in animal husbandry. Inscriptions mention Gopalgam reserved for this purpose. Curd, ghee, and butter were produced. Apart from cattle, chickens and goats were also reared.

Industries

There were many industries in ancient society:

  • Kamman — Iron workers
  • Kabara — Blacksmiths
  • Sawanakara or Thaladhara — Goldsmiths
  • Manikara — Gem cutters, gem miners
  • Dathika or Danthia — Tusk carvers
  • Kubakara or Kubkara — Potters
  • Thalavaya — Spinners

There are some terminologies used in trading:

  • Wanja, Wapara — Those who are engaged in trade
  • Poogaya — An organization in which traders work together

Some of the South Indian organizations that operated in Sri Lanka:

  • Nānādesi
  • Valangaiyar
  • Ayyarawar

Some inscriptions that provide information on trade affairs:

  • Inscription in the Western Aquatic Garden in Sigiriya
  • The Mandalagama inscription in Hambantota
  • The Weherakema inscription
  • Sorabora Wewa inscription
  • Boththgala inscription

Some of the harbors in Sri Lanka:

  • The Western Mathota harbor
  • Gokanna harbor
  • Jumbukola Pattana
  • Lakkapathiya

Professions in Ancient Sri Lanka

Some of the professions in ancient Sri Lanka:

  • Teaching
  • Acharaya
    • Those who taught crafts
    • Those who excelled in education
  • Hasthi Acharaya
    • Those who trained in taming elephants
  • Aswath Acharaya
    • Those who trained in horseback riding
  • Dunu Acharaya
    • Those who trained in archery
  • Doctors
  • Vedaya — Doctor
