Atoms

An atom is the smallest piece of an element that can be recognized as that element.

Atoms are made of three subatomic particles:

  • Proton
  • Neutron
  • Electron

The number of protons in an atom's nucleus is called its atomic number or proton number.

The mass number (sometimes known as the nucleon number) counts the total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus.

A molecule is a combination of two or more atoms chemically bonded together.

Atoms with the same atomic number but different relative atomic masses are called isotopes.

Relative atomic mass is the average mass of an atom, taking into account the amount of each isotope present in a naturally occurring sample of an element.

Atoms are electrically neutral (they have no overall charge). The charge on a proton (+) is equal but opposite to the charge on an electron (-); therefore, the charge in the atom is 0.

Number of protons = Number of electrons.

The search for patterns in chemistry led to the creation of the periodic table.

The vertical columns are called groups.

The columns are numbered from 1 to 7, and the eighth column is numbered 0.

You can use the periodic table to work out the number of protons, electrons, and neutrons in an atom.

The number of neutrons in an atom = mass number - atomic number.

The electrons move around the nucleus in a series of levels called energy levels or shells:

  • 1st energy level = 2
  • 2nd energy level = 8
  • 3rd energy level = 18
  • 4th energy level = 32

The first energy level is closest to the nucleus.

The arrangement of electrons in an atom is called electronic configuration.

Elements in the same group in the periodic table have the same number of electrons in their outer shells.

The number of electrons in the outer shell is the same as the group number for groups 1 to 7.

The period number corresponds to the number of occupied shells or the highest occupied shell.

All the elements in group 1 react vigorously with water to form hydrogen and hydroxides with similar formulas:

  • LiOH
  • NaOH
  • KOH

The group 0 elements are known as noble gases.

They are non-reactive.
